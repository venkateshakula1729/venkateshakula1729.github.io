About

Hello, fellow optimiser. I am Venkatesh, a Computer Science graduate from IIT Kanpur. I have been a math fanatic from an early age, which has shaped how I approach problem-solving, pushing me toward clean abstractions, efficient algorithms, and rigorous thinking rather than quick fixes.

During my 4 years at IIT Kanpur, I worked extensively across systems, machine learning, and software development through courses and projects. I find great satisfaction in optimising code paths, reducing latency, and testing new ideas. I am looking to go deep on hard technical problems, particularly in ML, systems, and quantitative research.

Outside of that, my time goes to lifting, reading, travelling, chess, badminton, and writing on this blog. This website serves as my digital logbook, a place to document what I'm learning, what I've built, and the occasional loose thought that survived long enough to become a post.

The best way to reach me is by email. I try to read and respond to most emails, but it helps if you follow this cold email advice.

💡 Fun Fact: My birthday falls on April 26th, the same day the legendary mathematician Srinivasa Ramanujan passed away in 1920. It is purely a coincidence, but as someone who spent their early years grinding for the Math Olympiad and running math clubs, I've always found it to be a great source of motivation when staring at tough problems.
