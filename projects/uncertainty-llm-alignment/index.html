Research Project

Uncertainty-Guided Test-Time Alignment for LLMs

Mentored by: Prof. Sayak Ray Chowdhury · IIT Kanpur · 2025

Overview

Large Language Models are typically aligned during training via RLHF or DPO, but these methods bake in a fixed reward signal. At inference time, we often want to adapt the model's behavior based on the specific context and risk tolerance of the deployment scenario. This project tackles that gap.

What We Built

We designed and implemented a risk-aware test-time alignment framework that leverages ensemble reward models to estimate both expected reward and the associated uncertainty for any given LLM output. The core insight: if multiple reward models disagree on the quality of a response, that disagreement itself is a useful signal.

The end-to-end inference pipeline supports three uncertainty-aware decoding strategies:

  • Uncertainty-aware Best-of-N (BoN) — Generate N candidates, rank by expected reward minus a risk penalty proportional to uncertainty
  • SoftBoN — Soft-weighted sampling from candidates using temperature-scaled reward-uncertainty scores
  • Sequential BoN — Iterative refinement: generate, evaluate, re-generate conditioned on the best candidate so far

Evaluation

We evaluated on GSM8K (grade school math) and MATH500 (competition-level mathematics), comparing our uncertainty-aware strategies against vanilla BoN and greedy decoding. Key components of the evaluation:

  • Reward normalization — Ensuring ensemble scores are comparable across different reward model architectures
  • Uncertainty analysis — Correlating reward model disagreement with actual generation quality
  • Ablation experiments — Isolating the effect of ensemble size, risk parameter, and N on downstream accuracy

Key Takeaways

The framework demonstrates that uncertainty estimation at test time is not just theoretically appealing but practically useful. On MATH500, uncertainty-aware BoN with a tuned risk parameter outperformed vanilla BoN by a meaningful margin, particularly on harder problems where reward model calibration matters most.

The most surprising finding: sequential BoN with uncertainty gating sometimes outperforms generating more candidates (higher N) with vanilla BoN, suggesting that smart candidate selection beats brute-force generation.
