Real Me

On losing the spark that once drove everything, and hoping it's just a pause in the song.

A boy who once chased every star, Who solved the equations, no matter how far. Math was a game, a beautiful fight, A spark in the mind that conquered the night. The ranks and the medals, the endless pursuit, A mind so rigorous, sharp and acute. But now in the silence, the screen glowing pale, The focus is slipping, the spirit feels stale. The fire that drove me, the burning desire, Has dimmed to an ember, a quiet, cold fire. I search for the spark, the hunger I knew, The enthusiastic boy, to himself ever true. Perhaps it's a phase, a pause in the song, But I miss the old me, where I truly belong.
