Campus Life at IIT Kanpur: Peacocks, Bulla, and Late Night Coding

Published: June 2026 ⏱ 6 min read

There's a specific kind of isolation that comes with living at IIT Kanpur. Located significantly outside the main city, the campus functions as its own self-sustaining ecosystem. You don't leave campus unless you absolutely have to. Over four years, this isolation creates a very distinct culture.

The Wildlife

The first thing anyone notices about IIT Kanpur is the peacocks. They are everywhere, and they are incredibly loud, especially right before a mid-sem exam when you're trying to sleep at 5 AM. Then there are the monkeys, who operate with the coordination of a highly trained heist crew, specifically targeting unlocked hostel rooms for Maggi packets.

The "Bulla" Culture

In the local IITK dialect, "bulla" refers to aimless, philosophical, or just completely unhinged conversations that happen late at night. You start by discussing an assignment, and by 4 AM you're debating the societal implications of general artificial intelligence or why a certain sorting algorithm is aesthetically superior to others.

These bulla sessions happen at MT (Motor Transport), at the OAT (Open Air Theatre), or just in a random hostel corridor. Looking back, some of the best intellectual growth I experienced didn't happen in the lecture halls of the CSE department, but during these 3 AM corridor discussions.

The Academic Pressure

Let's not sugarcoat it: the academic rigor is brutal. The transition from being a top ranker in JEE to suddenly being surrounded by people who are just as smart, if not smarter, is a humbling experience. There's a constant underlying pressure.

But there's a shared camaraderie in that suffering. When a professor drops an impossible assignment, the entire batch bands together. You learn the true meaning of collaboration when you're sitting in the lab at 2 AM trying to figure out why your NachOS implementation is throwing a segmentation fault.

Looking Back

As I wrap up my time here, the things I remember aren't the grades or the specific details of the CS courses. I remember the late-night cycles to the airstrip, the intense table tennis matches in the hall, and the feeling of finally getting an Accepted verdict on Codeforces after an hour of debugging.

IIT Kanpur gives you a lot of freedom, maybe more than an 18-year-old knows what to do with. How you use that freedom dictates your four years here. For me, it was the perfect sandbox to figure out what I actually liked doing.
